About Me

Get to know me better

After earning my Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine Degree from the New York College of Osteopathic Medicine in 2002, I completed my internal medicine residency at Lenox Hill Hospital in 2005. Over the past two decades, my journey in medicine has been both profound and enlightening. Early in my career, I recognized that effective treatment extends beyond addressing symptoms—it involves understanding the whole person. This realization led me to explore integrative medicine. I attended conferences and participated in workshops to deepen my knowledge. Gradually, I began incorporating holistic approaches into my practice, especially when managing chronic conditions.

My training as a Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine (DO) has been instrumental in this evolution. The osteopathic philosophy emphasizes the interconnectedness of the body's systems and the importance of treating the patient as a whole. This perspective has enriched my approach, allowing me to consider not just the physical aspects of illness, but also the emotional, lifestyle, and environmental factors that influence health. In my experience, every patient is unique. By taking the time to understand each individual's story, values, and circumstances, I believe we can chart a more successful path to healing.
